Declaration of Intent

I intend to improve our human environment.

Prior to the 2016 US presidential election, I launched a free public resource for public policy documents known as Congressional Research Service (CRS) reports. These reports, written by PhD's within the US Library of Congress, are created to benefit members of Congress & their staff; however, they are often accessed by lobbyists & industry leaders. By making CRS reports more easily available to researchers, the public, journalists, and off-the-hill politicians, I aspired to elevate understanding and discourse of public policy in the US.

The aim of my next project congress.ai is to accelerate the development & results of artifically intelligent legislative assistants by providing researchers with programmatic access to political research created by parliamentary & congressional research from over 100 countries.

This compilation is admittedly flawed, but in an early era of nationalistic, sexist, racist datasets, this data represents the pinnacle of government research. Executive & ministry research materials are written with greater agenda bias than that of "the people's branch" (Congress & parliaments) & by including every nation's research, national bias will be offset. Researchers within the field of machine learning, artificial intelligence, and NLP (specifically those studying artificially intelligent legislative assistants) will no longer need to focus on data collection and can be freed to focus on creating practical advances to humanity.
